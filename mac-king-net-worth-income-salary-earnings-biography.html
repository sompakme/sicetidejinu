    Mac King (born December 2, 1959) is a comedic magician who has appeared as a co-host on many television programmes. In the Clint Holmes Theater at Harrah’s Las Vegas, he performs his own family-friendly performance, “The Mac King Comedy Magic Show.” In portions known as the Mac King School of Magic, King taught the audience a small effect before and after each commercial on NBC’s The World’s Greatest Magic programmes. He is the only magician who has appeared in all five specials on the network. King plays at over 60 exclusive business events each year, in addition to his TV specials and Harrah’s appearances.  Goldfish, Fig Newtons, and a yellow raincoat that he says is a magical cloak of invisibility are among his most well-known effects. While executing his cut-and-restored rope act, one of King’s most unforgettable events happened. Some magicians use dull scissors, which is one of King’s pet peeves, and he makes it a point to maintain his scissors razor sharp. He accidently cut the tip of his thumb while doing the act. He wrapped his thumb with tape to try to stop the bleeding, but the damage was too serious. He asked the crowd if there was a nurse there, and thankfully, one stepped forward. She was able to bring the bleeding to a halt. Before heading to the hospital to have his injuries treated, King proceeded with his performance and completed his second concert later that evening. Following each concert, King meets audience members as they leave, signing signatures on photographs or books. “I’m Mac King,” he usually writes on his signature.
