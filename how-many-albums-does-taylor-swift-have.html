    Taylor Swift, the critically acclaimed singer-songwriter, has amassed an impressive discography throughout her career. From her early country-pop sound to her more recent pop-oriented albums, Taylor’s music has touched the hearts of millions of fans around the world.

    So, just how many albums does Taylor Swift have? Let’s dive into her discography and explore the plethora of musical gems she has delivered over the years.

    Taylor Swift’s Original Studio Albums

    Taylor Swift has released 10 original studio albums throughout her career. These albums showcase her growth as an artist and highlight her unique sound and style. Let’s take a closer look at Taylor Swift’s original studio albums:

    Taylor Swift (2006)

    Taylor Swift’s self-titled debut album, “Taylor Swift,” was released in 2006. It marked the beginning of her musical journey and featured hits like “Tim McGraw” and “Teardrops on My Guitar.”

    Fearless (2008)

    “Fearless,” Taylor Swift’s second studio album, was released in 2008. It catapulted her to international fame and included chart-topping songs such as “Love Story” and “You Belong with Me.”

    Speak Now (2010)

    “Speak Now,” released in 2010, showcased Taylor’s songwriting prowess and personal storytelling. Hits from this album include “Back to December” and “Mean.”

    Red (2012)

    2012 brought us “Red,” an album that saw Taylor Swift experimenting with new sounds and genres. It featured songs like “We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together” and “I Knew You Were Trouble.”

    1989 (2014)

    In 2014, Taylor Swift released “1989,” a pop-infused album that solidified her standing as a global superstar. It included chart-toppers like “Shake It Off” and “Blank Space.”

    Reputation (2017)

    “Reputation,” released in 2017, showcased a darker and more mature side of Taylor Swift. Hits from this album include “Look What You Made Me Do” and “Delicate.”

    Lover (2019)

    2019 brought us “Lover,” an album filled with love, vulnerability, and catchy pop tunes. It included songs such as “ME!” and “You Need to Calm Down.”

    Folklore (2020)

    “Folklore,” released in 2020, surprised fans with its indie-folk sound and introspective lyrics. Hits from this album include “Cardigan” and “Exile.”

    Evermore (2020)

    Later in 2020, Taylor Swift released “Evermore,” a sister album to “Folklore.” It continued the introspective themes and featured collaborations with artists like Bon Iver and HAIM.

    Midnights (2022)

    Most recently, in 2022, Taylor Swift released “Midnights,” an album that showcased her continued musical evolution and knack for writing relatable songs.

    Each of these original studio albums contributed to Taylor Swift’s incredible success and cemented her status as one of the biggest names in the music industry.

    Taylor Swift’s Re-recorded Albums

    In addition to her original studio albums, Taylor Swift has also been re-recording her early albums. These re-recorded albums, known as Taylor’s Versions, allow Taylor to reclaim control over her music and connect with her fans in a whole new way.

    So far, Taylor has released four re-recorded albums:

    • Fearless (Taylor’s Version) (2021)
    • Red (Taylor’s Version) (2021)
    • Speak Now (Taylor’s Version) (2023)
    • 1989 (Taylor’s Version) (2023)

    These albums feature updated versions of Taylor’s beloved songs, giving fans a fresh perspective on her early music. The re-recordings also serve as a testament to Taylor’s artistic growth and allow her to express her artistry on her own terms.

    Through Taylor’s Versions, Taylor Swift continues to captivate audiences with her timeless lyrics and captivating melodies, ensuring that her music remains a significant part of her artistic legacy.

    Taylor Swift’s Live Albums

    Throughout her career, Taylor Swift has delighted fans with electrifying live performances. In addition to her studio albums, Taylor has also released several live albums that capture the essence and energy of her concerts. These live recordings allow fans to experience the magic of Taylor’s live performances from the comfort of their own homes.

    Here are some of Taylor Swift’s notable live albums:

    • “Speak Now World Tour – Live” (2011): This album features live recordings from Taylor’s “Speak Now World Tour,” showcasing her captivating stage presence and vocal abilities.
    • “Live from Clear Channel Stripped 2008” (2020): In this album, Taylor delivers stripped-down performances of her hit songs, allowing her raw talent and songwriting skills to shine.
    • “Folklore: The Long Pond Studio Sessions” (2020): This live album presents intimate and acoustic renditions of the songs from Taylor’s critically acclaimed album “Folklore.” The recordings were gathered during a special studio session.
    • “Lover (Live from Paris)” (2023): This album captures Taylor’s epic performance in Paris during her “Lover” world tour. It showcases the grandeur and spectacle of her live shows.

    These live albums not only showcase Taylor Swift’s extraordinary talent, but they also provide a glimpse into the connection she shares with her audience during her live performances.

    Taylor Swift’s Reclamation of Her Masters

    One of the ongoing challenges Taylor Swift has faced throughout her career is the dispute over the ownership of her masters. The term “masters” refers to the original recordings of her songs, which are owned by the record label. In 2019, a highly publicized controversy arose when her original label, Big Machine Records, was sold to Scooter Braun’s company, Ithaca Holdings. This transaction resulted in Scooter Braun gaining control of Taylor’s early recordings, including her critically acclaimed albums.

    “For years I asked, pleaded for a chance to own my own work. Instead, I was given an opportunity to sign back up to Big Machine Records and ‘earn’ one album back at a time, one for every new one I turned in. I walked away because I knew once I signed that contract, Scott Borchetta would sell the label, thereby selling me and my future. I had to make the excruciating choice to leave behind my past. Music I wrote on my bedroom floor and videos I dreamed up and paid for from the money I earned playing in bars, then clubs, then arenas, then stadiums.”

    As a result, Taylor Swift decided to take matters into her own hands. She embarked on the ambitious task of re-recording her early albums, thus reclaiming her masters and regaining control over her music. This re-recording process involves creating new versions of her original songs, preserving the essence and spirit of the originals while imbuing them with a fresh perspective.

    The re-recording process allows Taylor to not only assert ownership and control over her music but also maintain the authenticity and integrity of her artistry. It gives her the power to decide how her songs are used and shared, ensuring that her creative vision remains intact.

    While re-recording an entire catalog of albums is undoubtedly a tremendous undertaking, Taylor Swift’s dedication to reclaiming her masters demonstrates her unwavering commitment to artistic control and the value she places on her art. It is a testament to her resilience and determination to protect her musical legacy, ensuring that future generations can experience her music on her terms.
