    In May 2024, TMZ reported that golf superstar Rory McIlroy filed for divorce from his wife of seven years, Erica Stoll. The news shocked many, especially with the timing being at the height of golf season. The notoriously private couple didn't make a joint statement on social media as many celebrity couples do, leaving fans' imaginations to run wild with theories and reasonings. The former couple share one daughter, Poppy McIlroy (4).

    According to Golf Monthly, McIlroy's net worth sits around $225 million. From tournament earnings, to endorsement deals, and investments, McIllroy has a lot to protect in this divorce. Follow along as we learn more about Rory McIlroy's divorce, marriage, and how the breakup could affect his career.

    Filing For Divorce Could Hurt Rory McIlroy's Chances At The PGA Championship

    TMZ reported that Rory McIlroy filed for divorce from Erica Stoll in Florida on Monday, May 13, 2024. Although McIlroy hails from the Ireland, the family currently lives in Jupiter, Florida, as noted by the US Sun. According to the Associated Press, the divorce filings shared that the former couple’s marriage was “irretrievably broken.” Furthermore, McIlroy’s legal team is adamant that the prenuptial agreement that both McIlroy and Stoll signed back in 2017 be put into force. The results of which could put his PGA Championship at risk and even harm his net worth.

    The ins and outs of the prenuptial agreement, however, are not open publicly at this time. If the penup is not iron clad, Stoll could walk away with millions.

    "Rory McIlroy's communication team confirmed today that a divorce has been filed. They stressed Rory's desire to ensure this difficult time is as respectful and amicable as possible."

    Woods' divorce cost him a fortune and played out in front of millions. As of this time, it's unknown if this divorce is amicable and wanted by both parties.

    One of the biggest reasons Rory McIlroy's divorce from Erica Stoll is so shocking is the timing of it all. McIlroy filed for divorce the same week he's to compete in the PGA Championship at Valhalla Golf Course. From Thursday to Sunday (May 16-19), McIlroy is to compete against golf's biggest names, like Scottie Scheffler and Brooks Koepka.

    How Did Rory McIlroy And Erica Stoll Meet?

    The Associated Press shared that Rory McIlroy and Erica Stoll had a rather unique start to their relationship. In 2012, Stoll worked for the PGA of America. During that year's Ryder Cup, Stoll and McIlroy met when he realized he set his alarm in the wrong timezone and was late for his match. Stoll was able to get McIlroy to his match on time after setting up a private police escort. In a shocking turn of events, he wound up winning his match, all thanks to Stoll.

    The two sparked a friendship because McIlroy was already in a serious relationship with professional tennis player Caroline Wozniacki. However, when McIlroy and Wozniacki broke it off, he and Stoll began dating shortly after. In 2015, the pair got engaged and were married two years later.

    In 2020, McIlroy and Stoll's family grew when they welcomed their daughter, Poppy Kennedy McIlroy. Similar to their relationship, they kept the majority of their pregnancy private until Stoll was about to give birth. Golfweek shared how thrilled McIlroy was to welcome his daughter.

    "It's a private matter, but we're really excited and can't wait for her to get here."

    Over the years, Stoll and Poppy have cheered on McIlroy and are his greatest supporters. Poppy was even her dad's golf caddy at the Master's Par 3 Contest, as noted by PEOPLE.

    Rory McIlroy Has Had A Complicated Road To Romance

    Divorcing Erica Stoll isn't the first heartbreak Rory McIlroy had to endure publicly. In 2013, McIlroy was engaged to tennis star Caroline Wozniacki. As professional athletes, the couple was beloved by sports fans. Unfortunately, a year later, McIlroy called off the engagement after the wedding invitations were already sent out.

    US Magazine shared McIlroy's statement regarding calling off the wedding and ending his relationship.

    "The problem is mine. The wedding invitations issued at the weekend made me realize that I wasn't ready for all that marriage entails. I wish Caroline all the happiness she deserves and thank her for the great times we've had."

    When all the dust settled, Wozniacki shared on In Depth with Graham Bensinger what happened and how the breakup affected her. "I was shocked. I thought at least it would be face-to-face or something," she said. "But there was nothing. It was a phone call and I didn't hear from him again."
